# C-Point Pilot — WHU Entrepreneurship Network

Quick prep sheet for the kickoff meeting. Four parts: what to decide, what we measure, what success looks like, how to keep it alive.

---

## 1. Decide in the meeting

- **How many members?** Launch number and semester peak. This picks the plan: Free (25) · L1 (75) · L2 (150) · L3 (250) · Enterprise (unlimited). If we undersize, invites get blocked mid-pilot.
- **Who runs it?** One owner + 1–2 admins (ideally student champions).
- **Steve AI on or off?** Owner add-on. If on, every member can use Steve in the community — great differentiator, decide upfront.
- **Money.** Free pilot, discounted plan, or 14-day trial then convert? Agree the decision point at pilot end.
- **How long?** One semester (8–12 weeks), review at the midpoint.

Keep it simple: **one network, invite-only, no sub-communities yet.**

### Before launch day (our side)

- [ ] Network created, plan set, Steve on/off applied
- [ ] Send a test invite to a real `@whu.edu` address — check it lands (not spam). Invites are the only way in.
- [ ] Member list received, or QR invite ready for the kickoff event
- [ ] App + web tested on the network
- [ ] 3–5 seed posts and a first event already live
- [ ] Success numbers agreed (section 3)

---

## 2. What the owner gets (analytics)

Every two weeks, we send a short **Network Health Report**:

- **Are people joining?** Invites sent → accepted → profile completed
- **Are they coming back?** Weekly active %, week-over-week retention
- **Are they participating?** Posts, comments, reactions, events, polls — and who the top contributors are
- **Is Steve being used?** (if on) Credits used and where

Always aggregates — never member-level surveillance. GDPR-clean.

**Coming during the pilot:** a self-serve owner dashboard with these same numbers. WHU gets to shape what it shows.

---

## 3. What success looks like

- **70%+** of invited members join
- **40%+** active each week by week 4
- **25%+** have posted or commented by mid-pilot
- **At the end:** owner feedback conversation + decision on the paid plan

If a number dips two weeks in a row → we step in with a fix (event, poll, champion push, fresh QR invite on campus).

---

## 4. Keeping engagement up

**Launch big.** Invite everyone at once around a kickoff event — QR code on a slide. Owner posts the founding post the same day.

**First two weeks = activation.** Intro thread, one poll ("what do you want from this network?"), first event with RSVP. Goal: every member completes a profile and does one thing.

**Then it's about habits.** 2–3 owner/admin posts a week, weekly poll, monthly event. Student champions as admins matter most — engagement follows admin activity almost 1:1.

**Steve fills the quiet.** If the package is on, teach members to @Steve in the feed — instant answers even when humans are offline.

**Don't split too early.** Add cohort/topic sub-communities only when one topic clearly overflows.

**What to demo for entrepreneurs:**
- Events with RSVP — pitch nights, speakers
- Polls — quick feedback, topic votes
- Pinned key posts — the resources founders keep asking for
- Networking page — "find a co-founder, find an investor"
